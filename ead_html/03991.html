Biographical/Historical note

Organized in January 1976, the Polish American Librarians Association (PALA) was created to help increase awareness of the needs of the Polish-American community through teachers, authors, publishers and librarians. The organization grew out of a workshop held at Queens College, City University of New York in 1975 attended by Victoria Gala, of Detroit, who became PALA's first president. A number of committees were begun within the organization, including a Bibliography Committee which provided lists of ethnic materials for elementary school and children's librarians.
